# Abstract

The LIMEN AI Edge-Case Atlas presents a systematic methodology for identifying, classifying, and documenting edge-case incidents in public AI systems. By integrating provenance metadata, a structured taxonomy, and an attestation envelope profile, the atlas generates machine‑readable evidence packages that link each incident to source records, jurisdictional context, and relevant regulatory frameworks. This paper introduces the attestation envelope profile v0.1, a JSON schema for packaging extraction logs, classification layers, and evidence‑role mappings. We also present a claim‑support matrix that maps research claims to evidence roles, and describe a clustering pipeline that groups incidents by taxonomy and provenance similarity. The resulting dataset, comprising over 150 edge‑case records in multiple languages, reveals evidence‑access bias, gaps in multilingual coverage, and challenges in cross‑jurisdictional comparison. These results provide a foundation for policy discussions, standards development, and further research into AI governance. We argue that structured evidence packages enable more transparent and reproducible assessments of AI governance gaps, supporting the development of robust, evidence‑based regulatory frameworks.
