# Negative Result: Baltic Language Claim-Support Validation

## Experiment Overview
- Goal: Validate claim-support linkage for a policy claim about Baltic AI regulation in the context of the LIMEN Edge-Case Atlas.
- Source(s) investigated: Public registers from Estonian, Latvian, Lithuanian government portals; OECD AI Principles translation; local AI ethics observatories.
- Method: Query source ledger, search for relevant documents, assess machine translation confidence, evaluate evidence tier.

## Findings
- No primary source documents directly addressing the claim were found in the public repositories.
- Machine-translated snippets returned low confidence (< 0.7) and lacked contextual nuance.
- Secondary sources mentioned the claim only in passing, without substantive evidence.
- Therefore, the claim-support linkage cannot be established with current public evidence.
- Uncertainty level: high.

## Implications
- The claim may need refinement or may be unsupported in the current evidence set.
- Highlights a gap in Baltic-language public evidence availability for AI governance claims.
- Suggests need for targeted source collection in Lithuanian or Latvian languages.

## Next Steps
- Expand source collection with local language experts.
- Re-evaluate claim with broader search after additional sources become available.
- Potentially flag as a “weak claim” in manuscript and move to “drop/rewrite” category.

**Status:** Negative result documented; no supporting evidence found.
