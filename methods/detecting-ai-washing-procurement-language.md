# Methods Note: Detecting AI Washing in Public Tenders Through Procurement Language Analysis

## Objective
To systematically identify potential AI washing in public procurement documents by analyzing language patterns and metadata without requiring technical implementation review.

## Methodology
1. **Source Selection**: 
   - Public tender documents from EU/EEA jurisdictions
   - Non-English sources prioritized (Dutch, Finnish, French)
2. **Language Analysis**:
   - Keyword frequency analysis: "AI", "artificial intelligence", "machine learning"
   - Contextual examination: claims vs. actual technical specifications
   - Metadata crosswalk: Compare declared AI capabilities with vendor information
3. **Triaging**:
   - Tier 1: Clear technical specifications matching AI claims
   - Tier 2: Vague or aspirational AI language without technical detail
   - Tier 3: Discrepancies between AI claims and vendor capabilities

## Output
- Evidence-tier classification for each analyzed tender
- Crosswalk table linking procurement language to technical reality
- Recommendations for further verification of Tier 2/3 cases

## Limitations
- Does not assess actual implementation
- Relies on publicly available information
- Language analysis may miss nuanced technical descriptions

Feeds evidence-tier funnel and legal uncertainty matrix visualizations.
