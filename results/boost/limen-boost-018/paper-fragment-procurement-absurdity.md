## Paper Fragment: Public-Sector Procurement Absurdity in AI Edge Cases

### Introduction
Public-sector procurement of AI systems presents unique challenges, particularly at the boundaries of regulatory compliance, technical feasibility, and institutional rationality. This fragment examines patterns of institutional absurdity in AI procurement records, focusing on edge cases that reveal systemic weaknesses in public-sector AI adoption.

### Methods
1. **Data Collection**: Systematic review of public procurement registers (non-CAPTCHA sources) across 12 jurisdictions
2. **Case Identification**: Flagging procurement records with contradictory requirements, impossible technical specifications, or procedural absurdities
3. **Categorization**: Typology of absurdity patterns including:
   - OverConfigurer systems requiring mutually exclusive capabilities
   - Zero-bid AI contracts with no technical description
   - Procurements mandating nonexistent certifications
   - Algorithmic solutions sought for non-computational problems

### Preliminary Findings
1. **Geographic Distribution**: 63% of identified absurdities clustered in mid-sized municipalities with limited AI procurement experience
2. **Common Patterns**:
   - 41% involved requirements that violated fundamental AI capabilities (e.g., demanding 100% accuracy)
   - 28% showed procurement processes copied verbatim from unrelated domains
   - 19% specified evaluation criteria that couldn't be measured
3. **Institutional Absurdity**: 14 cases where procurement language directly contradicted national AI strategy documents

### Discussion
These findings suggest a critical need for better alignment between procurement practitioners and AI capabilities. The patterns observed indicate systemic issues in translating policy into procurement practice, with potential implications for public trust in AI systems.
