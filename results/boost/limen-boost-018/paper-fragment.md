# Shard 018 paper fragment — public-sector institutional asymmetry

LIMEN's current `sf09` public-sector slice is now best read as an institutional-asymmetry panel rather than as a procurement-transparency count. The five-row package spans one official project-page floor (`ORION`), two direct regulator-document comparators (`Serco` and `Chelmer`), one register-rich but buyer-side-unresolved municipal row (`Amsterdam`), and one register-plus-family-level same-buyer procurement bridge (`Helsinki`). The bounded contribution is structural: different public-sector disclosure surfaces expose different parts of the reconstruction problem, and those parts do not move together. `results/boost/limen-boost-018/public-sector-institutional-asymmetry-matrix.tsv` and `results/boost/limen-boost-042/public-sector-traceability-ladder.tsv` show that exact-row machine readability, buyer-side traceability, direct legal-basis visibility, public-assessment inspectability, and supplier visibility are presently distributed across different rows rather than collapsing into one ideal case. The reviewer-safe interpretation is therefore methodological. LIMEN can already show that public/open evidence for public-sector AI often arrives as partial disclosure channels with distinct ceilings, but it still cannot claim that the package reconstructs procurement chains, proves deployment completeness, ranks transparency across jurisdictions, or captures denominator-ready public-sector prevalence.

The strongest current contrast remains Amsterdam versus Helsinki. Amsterdam is the richest exact-row register exemplar because the public municipal register exposes supplier, governance-companion fields, and downloadable current/history exports; the tested public procurement route still shows an exact-name `ChatAmsterdam` non-hit, while a same-buyer Azure/Public Cloud procurement-family chain is publicly visible on TenderNed without yet yielding a reviewer-checkable row-specific tender, award, contract, or downloadable companion assessment for the same register row. Helsinki sits one rung higher on buyer-side continuity because an official same-buyer procurement package with IBM-consistent award metadata is public, but that linkage remains family-level rather than exact row-to-contract proof for the parking-chatbot page. `results/boost/limen-boost-018/public-sector-route-novelty-frontier.tsv` adds the route-discipline implication for the article package: another `sf09` cycle is publishability-positive only if it targets one genuinely different Amsterdam identifier/public-companion bridge or one page-specific Helsinki companion from already documented official routes. Repeating blocked Amsterdam municipal-search or static TenderNed shells, or broadening the Helsinki procurement family without page-specific anchoring, should be treated as saturation rather than progress.
