## What the matrix adds

The matrix converts six existing multilingual leads into a reviewer-legible source-governance artifact with explicit fields for:

1. outlet/source authority class;
2. authority band (`high`, `medium_high`, `medium`, `medium_low`);
3. current evidence ceiling for manuscript use;
4. whether a direct source URL has been resolved locally;
5. the main blocker to claim strengthening;
6. the next authority-upgrade move;
7. explicit negative-result notes when only Google News redirect evidence exists.

## Paper-readiness delta

- Upgraded LIMEN's multilingual family from a loose lead queue to a compact source-authority instrument that a reviewer can inspect before trusting any non-English case package.
- Made the authority gradient visible across multilingual discovery surfaces: one official police-source lead, one public broadcaster, several reputable/regional media surfaces, and their distinct claim ceilings.
- Added dashboard-ready hooks for a jurisdiction/language coverage panel, lead-to-authority upgrade funnel, source-diversity panel, education anomaly timeline, fraud-modality panel, and public-sector AI deployment map.
- Reduced the risk that multilingual title-gloss leads are mistaken for paper-grade incident truth.

## Claims verified, rewritten, or dropped

What this cycle now supports:
- LIMEN can make a cautious methods/data claim that its multilingual discovery package has explicit outlet-authority scoring and upgrade paths rather than undifferentiated media leads.
