## What the artifact adds

The TSV compares three public-sector rows across the same bounded questions: what surface exists locally, whether it is direct-source resolved, whether it shows project or deployment stage, whether notice/legal-basis text is present, and whether any procurement/register trace is locally attached.

It deliberately records a negative result that matters for publication: LIMEN now has one strong multilingual official project page and two UK document-grade regulator packages, but zero local rows in this mini-family yet include an attached procurement notice, buyer-side contract record, or public AI/register disclosure trace.

## Paper-readiness delta

- Gives the first compact table-ready comparison for LIMEN's `sf09` family that distinguishes project-page evidence from regulator-enforcement evidence without collapsing either into procurement/register disclosure.
- Makes the public-sector evidence gap visible as a structured absence rather than vague prose.
- Strengthens methods and limitations language for any dashboard or paper section that discusses public-sector AI disclosure asymmetry, especially across multilingual and regulator-led rows.
- Keeps claims conservative: the new table is about disclosure-surface type and current local support depth, not about deployment legality, procurement irregularity, or completeness of public disclosure outside the local package.

## Claims verified, rewritten, or dropped

What this cycle now supports:
- LIMEN can show that its current public-sector mini-family is heterogeneous: one official multilingual project page plus two direct regulator-document packages.
