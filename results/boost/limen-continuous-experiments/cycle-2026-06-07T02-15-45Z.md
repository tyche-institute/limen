## What the artifact does

The TSV converts the four-row public-sector comparison into a figure/table-ready completeness matrix across the most manuscript-relevant fields:

- whether the source is direct-source resolved;
- whether a procurement or register trace is present;
- whether explicit legal-basis text is visible;
- whether deployment-stage signals are visible;
- whether human-review/governance signals are visible;
- whether supplier and impact-assessment signals are visible.

## Core result

The matrix makes one reviewer-important asymmetry explicit:

1. The two UK regulator-document packages expose legal-basis and operational-stage signals, but still do not expose buyer-side procurement or register traces.
2. The Slovenian police project page exposes project purpose and governance framing, but remains project-page-limited for procurement, supplier, and operational-stage reconstruction.
3. The Dutch algorithm-register row is the only local row that currently exposes a true disclosure/register trace, named supplier signal, and impact-assessment signal, but it still points to a linked internal source rather than a fully reconstructable public implementation record.
