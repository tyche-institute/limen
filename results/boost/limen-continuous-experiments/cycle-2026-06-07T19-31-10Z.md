# LIMEN continuous experiments cycle — CPU mining shard yield audit

Timestamp: 2026-06-07T19:31:10Z
Lane: `limen-continuous-experiments`
Sprint context: hostile-reviewer pass

## Why this experiment

The project already has repeated CPU-mining runs across six shard families. Under the sprint directive, the next smallest publishability move is not another blind mining pass but a methods-grade check on whether each shard is actually yielding LIMEN-local material rather than cross-project contamination from Atlas, GAIA, PALLAS, and adjacent Tyche projects.

## Inputs

- `results/cpu-mining/*/*/summary.json` (latest and previous run per shard)
- project root `/srv/tyche/projects/limen-ai-edge-case-atlas`

## Main result

Three shards now look productive for local exploitation rather than further query tuning:

- `residual-weird` — `97.71%` LIMEN-local share
- `security-agentic` — `91.73%` LIMEN-local share
- `health-finance-education` — `88.10%` LIMEN-local share

Three shards now look dominated by cross-project contamination and should be retuned or paused before another local mining cycle:

- `public-sector` — `1.96%` LIMEN-local share; dominated by `/srv/tyche/pallas/pallas-ai-agent-observatory` (`47.90%` of all hits); top query `algorithm register`
- `identity-provenance` — `8.87%` LIMEN-local share; dominated by `/srv/tyche/gaia/tyche-research-vault` (`72.07%` of all hits); top query `C2PA`
- `legal-research` — `11.00%` LIMEN-local share; dominated by `/srv/tyche/gaia/tyche-research-vault` (`41.60%` of all hits); top query `AI evidence`

## Negative result recorded explicitly

Repeated mining did not materially improve LIMEN-local recall for `public-sector`, `identity-provenance`, or `legal-research`. The latest-versus-previous deltas are tiny (`+8`, `+18`, and `+12` LIMEN-local matches respectively) while the overall hit pools remain overwhelmingly external. That is useful negative evidence against treating those query families as efficient LIMEN discovery routes in their current form.

Exploit high-yield shards (`residual-weird`, `security-agentic`, `health-finance-education`) into candidate-case validation or dashboard-ready saturation views. Do not spend the next cycle on another unbounded mining pass across `public-sector`, `identity-provenance`, or `legal-research` unless the query family is narrowed to LIMEN-specific edge-case language.
