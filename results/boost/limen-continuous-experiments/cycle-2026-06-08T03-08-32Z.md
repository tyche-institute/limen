Method:
1. Took only the four `frontier_monitor_only` rows from the canonical reopen-gate board.
2. Joined them to the latest drift and exploitation tables to recover current totals, external matches, anchor-bearing local signal hits, and current signal-share baselines.
3. Computed per-query sensitivity measures that do not invent new evidence: signal-share gain from one additional anchor-bearing hit, signal-share gain from two additional anchor-bearing hits, and the minimum integer count of new anchor-bearing hits needed to move the signal share by +0.5 pp or +1.0 pp at the current denominator.
4. Ranked the four rows by easiest visible movement (`additional_signal_hits_needed_for_plus_1_0pp`, then current signal share) so later cycles can tell the difference between a genuinely visible frontier change and a numerically trivial one.
5. Kept the ceiling explicit: this board does not reopen any row, does not change case counts, and does not claim that a small share change alone is substantively important without a new external/public anchor.

Findings:
- The four monitored frontiers are not equally sensitive under their current denominators. Ranked by easiest +1.0 pp movement: procedural contamination (2 hits for +1.0 pp), synthetic identity (2 hits for +1.0 pp), tool misuse (6 hits for +1.0 pp), AI vulnerability (8 hits for +1.0 pp).
- Two rows are `fast_mover` frontiers (`2` rows need only 2 anchor-bearing hits for a +1.0 pp move), while `tool misuse` is already slower (`6` hits) and `AI vulnerability` is slowest (`8` hits) because its denominator is much larger.
- `procedural contamination` currently has the strongest visible baseline (`8.47` signal-share pct; `54.80` reusable-local-share pct), while `tool misuse` remains the thinnest monitored frontier (`1.98` signal-share pct; `0` visible external matches).
- The board converts the vague phrase "materially stronger anchor-bearing share" into a machine-readable denominator-aware check for later dashboard, methods, and reviewer-response reuse.

Negative result made explicit:
- Not every future frontier hit should be narrated equally. For the larger-denominator monitored queries, one extra anchor-bearing hit may still be too small to move the share visibly; future cycles should record that as thin movement rather than overstate it as discovery growth.
