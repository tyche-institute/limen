## Shared Bridge Grammar

When any manuscript, dashboard, or thesis chapter references more than one
observatory, the following grammar must hold:

1. **Layer, not corpus.** "LIMEN, GAIA, and PALLAS are complementary evidence
   layers, not one fused empirical corpus."
2. **Separate denominators.** Each layer names its own denominators. No
   sentence sums across the evidence-grade boundary or across observatory
   boundaries.
3. **Ownership attribution.** Each claim attributes its observatory:
   - "GAIA shows..." for public-evidence visibility claims
   - "PALLAS indicates..." for readiness/sidecar claims
   - "LIMEN documents..." for edge-case/taxonomy/proof-ceiling claims
4. **No fused total.** No sentence presents a count spanning all three
   observatories.
5. **No country ranking.** No sentence ranks countries based on any single
   observatory's evidence or on blended evidence.
6. **No deployment certainty.** No sentence infers deployment prevalence
   from public-evidence visibility.

## Prohibited Language (across all three observatories)

The following phrasings are prohibited in any manuscript, dashboard caption,
or thesis chapter that references the coordinated observatory program:

1. "The combined corpus of GAIA, PALLAS, and LIMEN contains N records"
2. "Country X ranks highest/lowest in AI governance based on our data"
3. "Our evidence shows N% of countries have deployed AI agents"
4. "The three observatories together demonstrate compliance/non-compliance"
5. "A total of N AI incidents across all observatories"
6. Any single denominator that sums across observatory boundaries
