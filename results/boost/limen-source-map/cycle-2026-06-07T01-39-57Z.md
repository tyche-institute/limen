## Why this route

The local project already had a usable source-family baseline, but the hostile-reviewer sprint and the LIMEN goal card make a finer publication-facing distinction necessary: named comparator sources need their own rows because they do not support the same claims.

## What changed

- Rebuilt `sources/source-family-ledger.tsv` from 12 coarse families into 15 reviewer-facing families.
- Split the comparator layer into separate AIID, OECD AIM, AVID, MITRE ATLAS, MIT AI Risk Repository, and CSET AI Harm rows.
- Preserved separate public-record families for regulator/enforcement, court/tribunal, and public-sector registers/procurement.
- Kept queue-only and planned states explicit for court records, CSET machine-ingest use, security-advisory role separation, social/web leads, and creative/normative outlier sources.
- Refreshed `sources/source-notes.md`, `results/source-map/status.md`, `journal.md`, and `next.md` to match the new family resolution.

## Paper-readiness delta

The LIMEN source map now answers a reviewer-grade methods question more clearly: which source families are incident monitors, which are technical/security comparators, which are conceptual taxonomies, which are public records, and which are discovery-only surfaces. That makes the first article package less likely to overstate what any one source family proves.

## Remaining blocker

No court-originating record is yet captured locally, and the public-sector exemplar still needs to become a comparative schema note rather than a single resolved case.
