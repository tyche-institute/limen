# GAIA / PALLAS / LIMEN bridge note — 2026-06-14

Date: 2026-06-14
Scope: article-program bridge only

LIMEN connects to GAIA and PALLAS as the edge-case accountability layer in a three-observatory program, but it does not share their denominator.

- GAIA asks what is publicly visible about agentic or public-sector AI systems.
- PALLAS asks how evidence-ready a country or source environment is for public-AI observability.
- LIMEN asks how edge cases, anomalies, and accountability-relevant incidents can be organized without overclaiming truth, legality, prevalence, or compliance from public reports.

The shared bridge is methodological, not count-fusing:
- denominator discipline;
- caption grammar;
- source-authority and translation caution;
- claim ceilings;
- reviewer-safe explanation of why public visibility, country evidence-readiness, and edge-case accountability are complementary but non-interchangeable.

Therefore:
- GAIA counts must not be reused as LIMEN case denominators;
- PALLAS readiness bands must not be reused as LIMEN incident maturity bands;
- LIMEN edge-case counts must not be reused as country rankings or deployment proxies.

What can be shared safely:
- limitations language;
- observatory design logic;
- proof-ceiling discipline;
- methods appendix material on denominator control and source asymmetry.

Bottom line: LIMEN belongs in the same article program as GAIA and PALLAS, but only as a third evidence layer with its own bounded denominator and its own proof ceiling.
