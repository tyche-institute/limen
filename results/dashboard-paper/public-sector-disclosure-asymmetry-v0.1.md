## Bounded result

The strongest current LIMEN public-sector rows point to four different disclosure surfaces rather than one generic transparency class:

- a Slovenian official project page;
- two UK regulator enforcement/reprimand packages;
- two municipal public AI-register entries, one Dutch and one Finnish;
- one Danish regulator decision page used as a rare-language governance sidecar.

These surfaces expose materially different fields. In the current local package, the Dutch register row is richest for visible metadata and governance companions and now also has public current+history CSV exports for the exact register row, the Finnish register row adds a second municipal register exemplar with public supplier, human-oversight, and risk-management sections plus a family-level same-buyer procurement companion, the UK regulator rows are strongest for quoted legal-basis and remediation language, the Danish regulator decision page adds a bounded legal-basis-governance anchor without buyer-side traceability, and the Slovenian project-page row remains weakest for reconstruction beyond project existence and stated purpose. This supports a methods/results claim about disclosure-surface heterogeneity. It does not support claims that missing procurement traces prove hidden deployment, illegality, procurement misconduct, or compliance certification.
