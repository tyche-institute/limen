## Reviewer-safe manuscript paragraph

LIMEN's current multilingual package is already useful as an evidence-maturity and observability result, but not as a flat count of non-English incidents. The six-row queue currently splits into one bounded substantive direct-official row, two bounded appendix/methods rows, and three queue-only limitations rows, with translation-review metadata still required for all six rows. The strongest substantive multilingual exemplar remains the Slovenian official-police-led ORION row, now strengthened by an EU fact-sheet companion plus partner-side public companions from the ORION project site and UIC notices, while the Finnish Yle and Slovenian Radio Ptuj rows are better read as bounded comparators showing that some under-covered-language public sources can become reviewer-usable without becoming official corroboration. The unresolved rows are also heterogeneous in a publication-relevant way: Finland/Kaleva now sits at a stronger preview-text-visible direct-article-shell state, whereas the Lithuanian and Estonian rows remain high-undercovered blocked-route evidence under mixed rights/access and challenge-gating constraints. The contribution is therefore structural rather than geographic-prevalence-based: LIMEN can show how multilingual edge-case evidence matures unevenly across authority, accessibility, and translation burden, while keeping weaker leads visible as explicit limitations rather than silently dropping them.

## Methods / limitations paragraph

The multilingual package should be narrated through route states, not through language labels alone. Lithuania and Estonia are presently useful because they document compliant stopping conditions for hard-to-reach smaller-language sources: one row remains tied to a journalistic police-warning frame with unresolved AI modality under a mixed rights/access barrier, and one remains a challenge-gated education anomaly whose exact AI role is still unverified. Finland/Kaleva should not be collapsed into that same blocked category, because the public article shell already exposes bounded preview text and therefore supports a narrower but stronger unresolved state than title-gloss-only discovery. None of these rows should be used for final legal, policy, deployment, prevalence, or adjudicative claims. Their present value is methodological and evidentiary: they make the atlas's source ceilings, route validity checks, translation caution, and rare-language coverage limits inspectable to reviewers.
