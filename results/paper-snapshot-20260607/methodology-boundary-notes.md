# Methodology and boundary notes

## Route A framing

The paper should be written as a public-source observatory methods/data paper. The contribution is the evidence posture: source-family saturation, evidence tiers, duplicate control, translation caution, legal uncertainty, caption claim ceilings, and dashboard-ready uncertainty surfaces.

The atlas is not the list of cases. The atlas is the map of what public evidence lets LIMEN responsibly say.

## Claim ceiling

Allowed central claim:

`LIMEN demonstrates a reproducible public-source method for structuring heterogeneous AI edge-case evidence under explicit claim ceilings.`

Disallowed claims:

- AI harms are increasing.
- The corpus is complete.
- One jurisdiction, company, sector, or source family is worse than another.
- A system was unlawful unless a source is quoted only for its own procedural posture.
- A row proves compliance, certification, safety, efficacy, or legality.
- Complaint allegations are final findings.
- Media visibility is prevalence.

## Evidence handling

T3 authoritative rows can support narrow descriptive claims about what the source says, subject to source posture. T1 rows remain useful for discovery, limitations, and uncertainty visualization, but not for strong exemplars. Translation-dependent rows require paraphrase, original-language preservation, and low-friction caution labels.

DoNotPay and Workado are valuable official-text additions, but this snapshot holds them outside the 21-lineage headline denominator unless the manuscript deliberately adopts a synchronized post-FTC recomputation. The current safest route is sidecar-first.

## Limitations as contribution

The paper should foreground public-evidence limits as a scientific result:

- 403 and access barriers.
- Official records that summarize rather than expose court text.
- Court-text and caption-normalization gaps.
- Translation dependence.
- Duplicate and identifier-collision hazards.
- Media leads that cannot be promoted.
- Official records that still do not support legal conclusions.

These are not merely weaknesses. They are the observatory's reason to exist.
