# Methods and limitations skeleton v0.1

## Working title

LIMEN: A Public-Source Observatory for AI Edge Cases

## Abstract spine

AI incident and edge-case datasets often mix official records, media leads, vulnerability advisories, litigation summaries, research-integrity notices, and public-sector disclosures. LIMEN treats this heterogeneity as the object of study. We present a public-source observatory method that assigns evidence tiers, source-family roles, duplicate controls, translation cautions, legal uncertainty labels, and caption-level claim ceilings to a frozen snapshot of AI edge-case lineages. The result is not a prevalence estimate. It is an inspectable infrastructure for seeing what public evidence can and cannot responsibly support.

## Research questions

1. How can heterogeneous AI edge-case evidence be structured without laundering weak leads into false certainty?
2. Which source-family and document-depth distinctions matter most for public claims?
3. How do duplicate, translation, legal-posture, and access barriers affect dashboard-ready observability?

## Limitations as contribution

LIMEN does not solve public evidence incompleteness. It makes incompleteness visible and citable. Access barriers, summary-only official records, court-text gaps, translation dependence, duplicate collisions, and complaint/order posture limits are treated as data about observability.

## Narrow conclusion

The public-source atlas is not a claim that the listed events are representative. It is a method for preventing public-source evidence from being overread.
