## Residual promotion rule reaffirmed

Promote a new subtype or top-level label only if all conditions hold:

1. at least two distinct case lineages share the same mismatch against the current taxonomy;
2. the pattern cannot be expressed cleanly as a multi-label combination of current categories;
3. the new label improves at least one publication artifact;
4. the label can be defined with reviewer-legible inclusion and exclusion rules;
5. the support is not driven mainly by confidence-capped, translation-capped, title-gloss, inventory-only, denominator-choice-only, warning-page-only, sidecar-only, archive-only-singleton, or single-publisher-only rows;
6. for reviewed recurrence specifically, the shared failure mode must be concrete enough that distinct lineages can be compared without collapsing them into topic-only similarity; and
7. for boost-local leads specifically, duplicate-governance admission also requires the row to have a clear lineage role beyond mere thematic relevance or one-off source interest.
